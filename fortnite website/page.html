Fortnite Championships

There are a variety of Fortnite competitions .some are listed below:

  • Fortnite Champion Series (FNCS)
    The Fortnite Champion Series (abbreviated as FNCS), is the premier Tournament in Fortnite: Battle Royale. The inaugural FNCS was in Season X and has been held every season since. The tournament usually starts near the end of each season and is open to players ranked Champion League in Arena.
  • Fortnite world cup
    The Fortnite World Cup uses two of the game modes available to the video game, Fortnite. The main World Cup event as well as the Pro-Am use Fortnite Battle Royale, a battle royale game where up to 100 players airdrop onto an island without any weapons or armor, save for a pickaxe. Once on the ground, players must scavenge for weapons, armor, and healing items, as well as using their pickaxe to knock down existing features to gather wood, stone, and metal resources. This all must be done while avoiding attacks from other players, as well as staying within a shrinking circle on the map or risk taking fatal damage outside it. Players can use gathered resources to build walls, floors, and stairs and ramps to use as cover from attacks. The last player or team left alive wins.
  • Fortnite cups
    Fortnite Cups are competitive events hosted by Epic Games in the popular battle royale game Fortnite. These tournaments come in various formats and offer players the opportunity to compete for rewards such as in-game cosmetics, V-Bucks, or real-world prize pools, which can reach millions of dollars for major events.
    1. Cash Cups: Regularly scheduled tournaments where players can compete for cash prizes. These cups are often region-based and cater to various skill levels.
    2. FNCS (Fortnite Champion Series): This is the premier competition in Fortnite. It consists of several stages, including qualifiers and finals, with significant prize pools. The FNCS is held seasonally and is highly competitive.
    3. Platform Cups: Tournaments restricted to players on specific platforms, such as PlayStation, Xbox, or PC. This ensures fair competition between players using the same device type.
    4. Hype Cups: Non-cash tournaments that reward players with "Hype" points, which are used to rank up in the competitive ladder and qualify for higher-level tournaments.
    5. Collaborative Cups: Sometimes Fortnite partners with brands, content creators, or celebrities to create special themed cups, such as the Marvel Super Series or Icon Series tournaments, where participants can win exclusive skins and items.
